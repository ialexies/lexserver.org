## Not All Trends Are Worth Following

Every year, design blogs publish "Top 100 Web Design Trends" lists full of things that look great on Dribbble and break on a ₱1,200/month prepaid data connection.

This isn't that list.

Here are the trends we're actually implementing for clients in 2025 — the ones that are making a measurable difference for Philippine and Southeast Asian businesses.

## 1. Mobile-First, Not Just Mobile-Friendly

The difference matters. A mobile-*friendly* site is a desktop site that was tweaked to work on phones. A mobile-*first* site was designed for phones from the ground up.

In the Philippines, [over 73% of web traffic comes from mobile devices](https://www.statista.com). Designing for a 390px screen first — then expanding to desktop — produces significantly better results than the reverse.

**What this looks like in practice:**
- Large tap targets (minimum 44px)
- Single-column layouts that don't require horizontal scrolling
- Text that's readable without zooming
- Fast-loading images sized appropriately for mobile

## 2. Brutalist Typography (Done Right)

The trend that won't die — and for good reason. Large, bold headlines in display fonts create immediate visual hierarchy and are scannable on mobile in under a second.

The key is *restraint*. One display font for headlines, one readable font for body text. The mistake brands make is using 4 different typefaces and calling it "editorial."

We use **Archivo Black** for headlines and **Hind** for body text across most of our builds — a combination that's legible in Filipino, English, and mixed content.

## 3. Dark Sections, Not Dark Mode

Full dark mode is hard to maintain and often reduces conversion rates for service businesses. But strategic dark sections — a hero section on `#0a0a0a`, or a testimonials block on a dark background — create visual rhythm and make the white sections feel cleaner and more premium.

The contrast between dark and light sections guides the eye down the page naturally.

## 4. Fewer Animations, More Purpose

2023 was peak "scroll animation everything." Sections fading in, elements rotating, parallax backgrounds. It looked great. It also made pages feel slow and distracted users from converting.

In 2025, the best animations are purposeful: a single hero headline that fades in on load, a button that provides clear hover feedback, a menu that slides smoothly. Everything else should be instant.

**Rule of thumb:** If removing the animation doesn't hurt the user experience, remove it.

## 5. Clear, Specific CTAs

The generic "Contact Us" button is dead. Businesses that replace vague CTAs with specific ones see dramatically higher click rates:

- "Get a Free Quote" → 40% more clicks than "Contact Us"
- "Book a Free Call" → better than "Learn More"
- "Start Your Project" → better than "Hire Us"

This is not a visual design trend — it's a copy trend. But it lives in the design because button labels are design decisions.

## 6. Structured Proof Sections

Testimonials, client logos, metrics (15+ years, 50+ clients) — these need to be *designed* into the page, not appended as an afterthought. The most effective pattern we've found is:

1. Make a bold claim in the headline
2. Immediately follow it with proof (metric, quote, or logo strip)

Businesses in the Philippines that add this pattern consistently see better lead quality — because the people who contact you have already been sold before they reach the form.
